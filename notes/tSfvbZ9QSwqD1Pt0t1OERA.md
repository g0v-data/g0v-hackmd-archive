## 以色列團隊合作
:::info
嗨，有個以色列的團隊接洽，想要學習 vTaiwan 討論複雜議題的經驗（特別指名 UberX)，做為他們製作 digital deliberation tool 的基礎。
他們問了一些問題:
1) What criteria should be considered when determining the optimal number of participant clusters in a project like this?  / 參加公民審議的人數基準

There is no so-called limit of numbers of participants. In our experience, we priorities diversity over population coverage. But if you are expecting a huge online participation, I am intrigued to introduce people in Polis to you if that helps?  
- vTaiwan 在審議過程中，比較強調的或許是利害關係人的部分 rather than 人數。[name=peter]

![](https://s3-ap-northeast-1.amazonaws.com/g0v-hackmd-images/uploads/upload_ddf604416995b3080542f1a05d0afeec.png)
> 各種審議式活動大概的人數整理[name=Eli]

2) Could you elucidate how user actions within the application are reflected and translated into movements on the clustering graph?

Each statement store the data of how users ‘Agree’ ‘Disagree’ or ‘Pass’ on them. And that data is consisted as a summarisation of each users’s voting pattern.
Those users having similar voting patterns will gravitise closer and be reflected on the clustering graph as if sitting in the same cluster.
Those pieces of voting patterns are valuable information that defines whether a statement is a divisive or consensus statement. 

3) We remember you mentioning the use of Discourse for hosting conversations related to Pol.is surveys. How crucial do you find the role of comments and discussions on the platform in influencing the project's outcomes? How would you recommend implementing this ?

On the UX, Discourse still tracks timeline of a discourse clearer than Pol.is, simply because polis is alway capturing the most up-to-date collective intelligence, and leaving no clue on previous developments over the conversation. If you are for sure using polis, I recommend having a timeline management tool along with polis. You can use a website or Discourse t track the timeline, and announce Pol.is conversation’s start and end date on the timeline.

4) Did you have any concerns on the technological aspects of Pol.is's implementation? This includes imprecise or inadequate solutions you encountered.

Who will be answering the result of Polis is quite a critical element to have before evening staring the conversation. For example, is the result going to bring to the current ruling government and make clear decisions according to the polis conversation collected? That will largely affect everything, from how engaged the crowd will be, the amount of participation etc.  

5)did you find the ability to interact via comments through discord(By this we mean leaving a verbal text comment on a post ) as a useful attribute for the project ?  

- In some issues that need to dive deep, voting may post some limitations to the discussion. That's why we think that tools like Discourse can be a good place to make the discussion happen. [name=peter]
