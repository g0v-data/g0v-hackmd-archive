### What Is ISO Certification?

ISO certification is a formal approval that confirms a business follows specific international standards set by the International Organization for Standardization. These standards are designed to improve quality, safety, environmental responsibility, data security, risk control, and operational efficiency. Some of the most common ISO standards include:

-   ISO 9001 for quality management
-   ISO 14001 for environmental management
-   ISO 45001 for occupational health and safety
-   ISO 27001 for information security management
-   ISO 22000 for food safety management
-   ISO 22301 for business continuity management

Each standard focuses on a different area of business improvement. For example, ISO 9001 helps companies improve customer satisfaction and quality control, while ISO 45001 focuses on workplace health and safety. Choosing the right ISO standard depends on your industry, business activity, client requirements, and long-term goals.
