Haja Center is a creative hub in dedicated to empowering young people through "learning by doing." Founded in 1999 as a collaboration between the Seoul Metropolitan Government and Yonsei University, the center serves as a collaborative space where young people can explore interests like filmmaking, music, and design through hands-on projects. With a name derived from the Korean word haja (meaning "let’s do it"), the center supports young people to engage in self-initiated projects, craftsmanship, and community building, ultimately aiming to help them navigate their own career paths and foster a sustainable, creative ecosystem within the city.
