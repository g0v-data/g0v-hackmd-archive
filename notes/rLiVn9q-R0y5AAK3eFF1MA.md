### **Market Overview: Air Handling Units (AHUs)**

Air handling units (AHUs) play a crucial role in heating, ventilating, and air conditioning (HVAC) systems, ensuring the distribution and regulation of air within buildings. AHUs are responsible for filtering, heating, cooling, humidifying, and dehumidifying air, which improves the indoor air quality and maintains optimal environmental conditions. These systems are widely used in residential, commercial, and industrial settings, and their demand is increasing due to growing awareness of indoor air quality, energy efficiency, and climate control.

### **Market Segmentation**

#### **By Type:**

l **Packaged AHUs**: These units come pre-assembled with all necessary components, offering a cost-effective and space-saving solution. Packaged AHUs are commonly used in smaller commercial spaces and residential applications due to their compact size and ease of installation.

l **Custom AHUs**: Custom AHUs are designed for specific applications, offering flexibility in design, materials, and functionality. These units are often used in large commercial buildings, hospitals, and industrial facilities where unique requirements must be met.

l **Modular AHUs**: Modular air handling units are highly adaptable and can be assembled on-site, making them ideal for large-scale projects and installations where customization and flexibility are required.

l **Low Profile (Ceiling) AHUs**: These units are designed for installation in confined spaces, such as false ceilings. Low-profile AHUs are typically used in office buildings and residential apartments where space is limited.

l **DX Integrated AHUs**: DX (Direct Expansion) Integrated AHUs combine cooling and heating functions with air handling in a single unit, offering energy-efficient solutions for buildings with moderate temperature control requirements.

l **Rooftop Mounted AHUs**: Rooftop air handling units are commonly used in commercial and industrial buildings. These units offer a space-saving option and are particularly advantageous in buildings with limited indoor mechanical space.

l **Others**: Other types of AHUs include specialized units designed for unique applications, such as cleanrooms, pharmaceutical facilities, and other environments with strict air quality requirements.

#### **By Capacity:**

l **≤5,000 M3/H**: This capacity range is suitable for small residential and commercial spaces, such as single-family homes, small offices, and retail stores.

l **5,001 -- 15,000 M3/H**: These units are designed for medium-sized spaces, including small commercial buildings, restaurants, and educational institutions.

l **15,001 -- 30,000 M3/H**: AHUs in this capacity range are used in larger commercial buildings, hospitals, and medium-sized industrial facilities where higher air handling capacity is required.

l **30,001 -- 50,000 M3/H**: These units are designed for larger commercial and industrial facilities that require significant airflow, such as shopping malls, manufacturing plants, and large office complexes.

l **≥50,001 M3/H**: AHUs with this capacity are used in large-scale industrial applications, airports, data centers, and other environments that require extensive air handling and ventilation capabilities.
