# Interview with Bella Wexler
- 時間：2026/04/01 10:02
- 參與者：Bella Wexler, Peter
## 問題 list
### Background:
• First, could you please tell me a bit about yourself and your experience with vTaiwan and
any other civic tech projects or initiatives you contribute to?
• What motivated you to first get involved with these organizations?
• How would you describe your work to someone unfamiliar with it? For instance, what
responsibilities does typical work entail? What reputation does it have in Taiwan?
### Experience:
• How does your work with these initiatives tend to fit into Taiwan’s broader information
security ecosystem? For instance, how do these organizations relate to government
agencies and civic tech? Ex: Do you collaborate with moda or the Legislative Yuan?
• How, if at all, do these organizations relate to grassroots social movements or activism in
Taiwan? Ex: The Sunflower Movement in 2014?
• What challenges have you encountered in your research (if any) to expanding its
audience or reach in Taiwan? Why do you think this is?
• Have you applied any strategies that felt particularly effective to overcome challenges
and expand organizational reach?
### Opinions:
• I have heard from some people that political polarization impacts their work in civic tech
in Taiwan. Do you feel this way? Do you have any recent experiences in your work
where political polarization felt especially relevant?
• How would you describe the state of Taiwan’s information security ecosystem, especially
its defense against information manipulation?
• Do you feel that open data, fact-checking, and media literacy resources in Taiwan are
widely used by the general public?
• Has your opinion about the role of these resources evolved since you started
working/volunteering in this space?
• How are emerging technologies like generative AI impacting your work and challenges
Taiwan faces?
• To what extent do people trust civic tech resources in Taiwan? Probe: different degrees
for different people? Why do you think this is?
• How do you think Taiwan would be different if it didn’t have vTaiwan and the civic tech
community more broadly?
• Should people trust content posted by Taiwanese politicians or government officials ?
Why/Why not? Are there exceptions?

• If you worked for the Taiwanese government, such as in the Ministry of Digital Affairs,
what policies or initiatives would you try to implement to better stop the spread of digital
disinformation? How does this compare to what is being done now?
• Should civic tech work with government or independently?

### Looking to the Future:
• How do you view your role as an individual in Taiwan’s overall information security and
democratic resilience?
• What do you think Taiwan’s government can do better to counter the spread of
disinformation? What can the public do better? Probes: Strengths? Challenges/Setbacks?
• How do you think information security and media + AI literacy in Taiwan will change
over the next five years, compared to the last five? Probes: Time since Sunflower
Movement in Taiwan (including COVID) compared to novel impacts of generative AI...
• Are there any questions you think I should have asked you that I didn’t? Probes: any
more info to share? about your role, your beliefs about the state of Taiwan’s approach to
countering disinformation, concerns/hopes for the future? Corrections for me?
