Maleic Anhydride Market Expected to Reach USD 4.30 Billion by 2030, Driven by Growing Demand Across Construction, Automotive, and Textile Industries

The global [maleic anhydride market](https://www.snsinsider.com/reports/maleic-anhydride-market-2002), valued at USD 3.03 billion in 2022, is projected to reach USD 4.30 billion by 2030, expanding at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.5% over the forecast period from 2023 to 2030. Maleic anhydride, a versatile chemical intermediate primarily used in the production of unsaturated polyester resins (UPR), copolymers, and lubricant additives, is witnessing robust demand from diverse industries, including construction, automotive, textiles, and food & beverages. As the industrial sector pushes for advanced materials that enhance product performance and sustainability, maleic anhydride's unique properties position it as a critical component in several applications, fueling its global market growth.

**Market Drivers**

Several factors are driving the growth of the maleic anhydride market:

l **Increasing Demand for Unsaturated Polyester Resins (UPR):** Unsaturated polyester resins, widely used in construction, automotive, and marine industries, account for the largest application segment of maleic anhydride. These resins, recognized for their strength and resistance to corrosion and chemicals, are crucial in the production of fiberglass-reinforced plastics (FRP), pipes, tanks, and other composites. With the construction and automotive sectors experiencing rapid expansion, especially in emerging economies, demand for UPR and, consequently, maleic anhydride is expected to surge.

l **Rising Automotive Production and Lightweighting Trends:** Maleic anhydride-based copolymers and resins are being utilized increasingly in automotive manufacturing, particularly as manufacturers strive for lighter, more fuel-efficient vehicles. By enabling strong, lightweight composite materials, maleic anhydride applications support the trend toward vehicle lightweighting, which reduces fuel consumption and greenhouse gas emissions.

l **Growth in the Construction Sector:** Maleic anhydride's applications in construction materials, adhesives, and coatings make it a valuable component in modern infrastructure development. The ongoing urbanization and infrastructure investments, particularly in Asia-Pacific, North America, and Europe, are creating significant opportunities for maleic anhydride, as the demand for durable, high-performance construction materials continues to grow.

l **Advances in Biodegradable Polymers and Green Chemicals:** As environmental awareness intensifies, there is an increased demand for biodegradable polymers and other eco-friendly products. Maleic anhydride is being used in the formulation of eco-friendly materials and additives, driving research and development in sustainable chemistry. This shift towards sustainability is anticipated to foster maleic anhydride's application in industries like packaging and personal care.

l **Increasing Demand in Food & Beverage and Pharmaceutical Sectors:** Maleic anhydride's derivatives, such as malic acid and fumaric acid, are widely used in the food & beverage industry as flavoring agents, preservatives, and acidity regulators. Additionally, maleic anhydride's role in the production of specialty chemicals for the pharmaceutical industry is contributing to market growth, as these industries seek high-quality, safe, and effective chemical intermediates.

**Market Segmentation**

The maleic anhydride market is segmented by raw material, application, end-user, and region, each segment highlighting unique market dynamics and potential growth avenues.

### **By Raw Material**

l **N-Butane**: N-butane is the predominant raw material for maleic anhydride production due to its cost-effectiveness and efficient yield. It is primarily used in large-scale production facilities, contributing to a significant share of the market. The adoption of n-butane as a feedstock is particularly high in Asia-Pacific, where it is readily available and used by maleic anhydride manufacturers seeking to optimize costs.

l **Benzene**: Benzene is another raw material used in maleic anhydride production, although its application is gradually declining due to environmental regulations and health concerns. However, benzene-based production remains relevant in regions where the material's availability and infrastructure are conducive to its use, especially in certain parts of North America and Europe.

### **By Application**

l **Unsaturated Polyester Resin (UPR)**: UPR is the largest application segment, accounting for a substantial share of the maleic anhydride market. This segment is bolstered by high demand in construction and automotive applications where UPR's durability and cost-efficiency make it an ideal choice for a variety of products, including FRP, laminates, and coatings.

l **Copolymers**: Copolymers synthesized from maleic anhydride are finding increasing use in plastics, adhesives, and coatings due to their compatibility with various materials. The versatility of copolymers makes them suitable for a wide range of applications, especially in automotive manufacturing, construction, and packaging.

l **Lubricant Additives**: Maleic anhydride is an essential component in the production of high-performance lubricant additives that improve the efficiency and longevity of machinery. As industries prioritize operational efficiency, the demand for advanced lubricant additives is expected to rise, bolstering this segment's growth.

l **Alkenyl Succinic Anhydrides**: This application is gaining traction, particularly in the paper and textile industries, where alkenyl succinic anhydrides are used as sizing agents and modifiers. With the paper and textile industries experiencing steady growth, the demand for this application is expected to increase.

l **Malic Acid and Fumaric Acid**: Both derivatives are extensively used in food & beverage applications as acidulants and flavoring agents. The rising demand for processed foods and beverages, coupled with the growing pharmaceutical sector, is anticipated to drive demand for malic and fumaric acids.

### **By End-User Industry**

l **Construction**: The construction industry is the largest consumer of maleic anhydride-based products due to the high usage of UPR and other materials in building and infrastructure projects. With increased construction activity globally, particularly in emerging economies, demand in this segment is expected to remain strong.

l **Automotive**: Maleic anhydride applications in the automotive sector, particularly in lightweight materials and corrosion-resistant coatings, support the industry's shift toward energy-efficient vehicles. The rise in automotive production, coupled with the demand for fuel-efficient materials, is expected to bolster this end-user segment.

l **Food & Beverages**: In the food & beverage industry, maleic anhydride derivatives like malic acid and fumaric acid are popular as preservatives and flavoring agents. The growing consumer preference for processed foods and beverages is expected to drive demand in this sector.

l **Textile**: Maleic anhydride-based products are used as additives in the textile industry for their functionality in improving dye uptake and fabric durability. With the global textile industry expanding, especially in Asia-Pacific, demand in this sector is set to grow.

l **Pharmaceuticals**: Maleic anhydride is crucial in the production of specialty chemicals used in pharmaceuticals. As the pharmaceutical industry grows, particularly in response to global health challenges, demand for maleic anhydride in this sector is expected to increase.

l **Personal Care and Agriculture**: Maleic anhydride has applications in both personal care and agricultural sectors, where it is used in formulations for hair care, skin care, and crop protection products. The rise in demand for personal care products and modern agricultural solutions is projected to further support the market.
