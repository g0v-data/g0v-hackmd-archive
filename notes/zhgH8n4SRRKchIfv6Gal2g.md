## [steal ](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/zht/%E8%A9%9E%E5%85%B8/%E8%8B%B1%E8%AA%9E/steal)

        - They were so poor they had to steal in order to eat.
- idiom
    - steal sb's thunder
    - steal a march on sb
    - steal the limelight
    - steal the show/scene

### [steal sb's thunder](https://www.bbc.com/ukchina/trad/elt/english_now/2012/09/120914_todays_phrase_steal_someones_thunder.shtml)

- Sam stole my thunder when he said he'd done all the work.

### [steal a march on sb](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/zht/%E8%A9%9E%E5%85%B8/%E8%8B%B1%E8%AA%9E/steal-a-march-on-sb)

*If you steal a march on someone, you get an advantage over that person by acting before they do*
- Our rival company managed to steal a march on us by bringing out their software ahead of ours.

### [steal the limelight](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/zht/%E8%A9%9E%E5%85%B8/%E8%8B%B1%E8%AA%9E/steal-the-limelight)
