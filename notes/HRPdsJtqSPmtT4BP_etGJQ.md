You know, people talk a lot about sun signs, rising signs, all that jazz. But for me, it was always about the moon. Specifically, my moon in Virgo tangled up with a strong Cancer influence. And let me tell ya, that combination, it felt like having two different bosses in my head, constantly yelling at each other about how to process, well, everything.

My Virgo moon, bless its little analytical heart, always wanted to categorize every single feeling, put it in a neat little box, analyze its origins, and then, ideally, find a practical solution. It wanted to fix things, make lists, tidy up the emotional mess. It’s like, when I felt sad, my first instinct was to grab a mental whiteboard and start bullet-pointing the root causes, the triggers, and a five-point plan to get over it.

But then, my Cancer moon, that deeply sensitive, nurturing, and sometimes wildly moody part of me, would just… feel. It didn’t want a whiteboard; it wanted a warm blanket, a hug, and maybe a good cry in a dark room. It just wanted to be held, to feel safe and understood, not to be dissected like a lab frog. These two, they were a real piece of work together. It made for some internally wild times, I tell ya. One half of me screaming for order and logic, the other just wanting to curl up and not deal with a single thing.
