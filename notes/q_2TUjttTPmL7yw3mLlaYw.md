### **Market Overview**

Laminated glass consists of two or more layers of glass that are bonded together with an interlayer, typically made of polyvinyl butyral (PVB) or ethylene-vinyl acetate (EVA). This structure enhances safety and performance, making it a popular choice in the automotive industry. Laminated glass provides improved impact resistance, reduces noise levels, and offers UV protection, making it ideal for various applications in vehicles.

Sidelites, or side windows, utilize laminated glass to improve safety and reduce the risk of shattering upon impact. The aesthetic appeal of sidelites is also contributing to their growing popularity.

The sunroof segment is experiencing increased demand due to the rising consumer preference for panoramic roofs and enhanced in-car experiences. Laminated glass provides the necessary strength and protection while enhancing the vehicle's design.
