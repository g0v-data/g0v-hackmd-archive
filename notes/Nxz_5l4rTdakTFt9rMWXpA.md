**Regulatory Pressure Drives Feed Mycotoxin Detoxifiers Market.**

The feed mycotoxin detoxifiers market is increasingly driven by stringent regulatory pressures on controlling mycotoxin contamination in animal feed. Governments globally are implementing stringent regulations to protect animal health and ensure food safety. As per recent statistics provided by the European Commission, mycotoxins, such as aflatoxins and ochratoxins, have been subjected to strict regulations with the maximum allowable levels set at 2 µg/kg and 50 µg/kg, respectively, for feed meant for livestock. Similarly, in the US, the Food and Drug Administration has established how mycotoxin levels should be regulated in animal feeds. These regulations have been put in place to reduce the impact of mycotoxins, such as compulsory feed refusal, reduced production, and impacted animal health as the toxic compounds are known to bind to protein biosynthesis machinery. Therefore, feed producers are progressively integrating mycotoxin detoxification as part of the feed production process to comply with the set regulations and protect their livestock from contamination. Resultantly, the mycotoxin detoxifiers market is increasingly being characterized by a regulated focused market growth, which in turn creates a higher demand for innovative detoxification solutions.

**By Sub Additive**

Based on the rising demand for this segment and the availability of alternatives, the binders sub-segment held the largest share of the feed mycotoxin detoxifiers market in 2023. Binders have become an integral component of modern livestock management, and their popularity can be attributed to a range of reasons, such as effectiveness and cost-efficiency. Imaginatively, these binders work as a discrete shield within the digestive system of animals and not allow mycotoxins to enter the blood system. This is a very important function since without a proper shield, mycotoxin causes a range of neurological disorders, such as tremors, and in severe cases, it can even lead to death. In short, by binding with mycotoxin within the animals' digestive systems, binders prevent absorption and, thereby, protect livestock from a range of physical conditions.
