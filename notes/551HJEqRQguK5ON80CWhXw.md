### **What Are Engineered Plastics?**

Engineered plastics, also referred to as high-performance plastics, are a group of materials designed to provide superior mechanical, thermal, and chemical properties compared to commodity plastics. These plastics are often used in applications where the material must withstand demanding conditions, such as high temperatures, pressure, or corrosive environments.

Key types of engineered plastics include **Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene (ABS)**, **Polyamide (PA)**, **Polycarbonate (PC)**, **Thermoplastic Polyester**, **Polyacetal (POM)**, and **Fluoropolymer**. These materials are essential components in producing products and systems that require long-term durability, resistance to wear, and enhanced functionality.

### **Market Segmentation**

The engineered plastics market can be segmented by **type**and **end-use industry**:

#### **By Type****:**

1\. **Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene (ABS)**: Widely used in automotive, electronics, and consumer goods for its impact resistance, strength, and ease of manufacturing.

2\. **Polyamide (PA)**: Known for its durability, flexibility, and resistance to chemicals, used in automotive, industrial, and textile applications.

3\. **Polycarbonate (PC)**: Valued for its transparency, high impact strength, and heat resistance, making it essential for electronics and automotive parts.

4\. **Thermoplastic Polyester**: Used in industrial applications due to its excellent mechanical properties and resistance to environmental factors.

5\. **Polyacetal (POM)**: Offers high stiffness, low friction, and excellent dimensional stability, commonly used in gears and bearings.

6\. **Fluoropolymer**: Known for its resistance to chemicals and high temperatures, used in industrial and chemical processing applications.

7\. **Others**: Includes specialty plastics such as polyetheretherketone (PEEK), polyphenylene sulfide (PPS), and others, which are essential for advanced applications.
