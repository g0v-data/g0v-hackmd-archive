Unsaturated Polyester Resins Market Set to Surge, Projected to Reach USD 16.81 Billion by 2030

The global [**Unsaturated Polyester Resins (UPR) Market**](https://www.snsinsider.com/reports/unsaturated-polyester-resins-market-2392) is poised for substantial growth, with its market size valued at USD 10.31 billion in 2022. It is expected to reach USD 16.81 billion by 2030, representing a robust compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 6.3% during the forecast period from 2023 to 2030. This expansion is primarily driven by the increasing demand for lightweight and durable materials across various industries, including construction, marine, and transportation.

#### **Market Segmentation**

The Unsaturated Polyester Resins Market can be segmented into different categories based on type, end-use industry, and region:

**By Type:**

o **Orthophthalic Resin:** Widely used due to its excellent mechanical properties and cost-effectiveness, this resin type is prevalent in various applications, including construction and automotive.

o **Isophthalic Resin:** Known for its superior chemical and thermal resistance, isophthalic resin is commonly used in high-performance applications, especially in the marine industry.

o **Dicyclopentadiene (DCPD):** DCPD resins offer unique properties, making them ideal for applications requiring high impact resistance and weatherability.

o **Others:** This category includes Gelcoat resin, Terephthalic resin, and Chlorendic resin, which are utilized in specialized applications across multiple industries.

**By End-Use Industry:**

o **Building & Construction:** The largest segment, driven by the increasing demand for composite materials in infrastructure projects.

o **Marine:** The rising popularity of fiberglass-reinforced plastics in boat manufacturing is propelling this sector.

o **Transportation:** UPRs are used in various applications, including automotive parts and rail cars, contributing to their growing market share.

o **Pipes & Tanks:** UPRs are widely used in manufacturing durable and corrosion-resistant pipes and tanks, especially in the chemical and oil industries.

o **Electrical & Electronics:** The demand for lightweight and strong materials in electronic components is driving the adoption of UPRs.

o **Wind Energy:** With the increasing focus on renewable energy sources, UPRs are becoming essential in the production of wind turbine blades.

o **Artificial Stones:** The rising trend of using engineered stones for countertops and flooring is boosting the demand for UPRs.

o **Others:** This category encompasses various industries where UPRs are employed, highlighting their versatility.

l **Sustainability Initiatives:** As industries aim for sustainable solutions, the shift towards using composite materials made from UPRs that offer longer life cycles and reduced environmental impact is gaining traction.
